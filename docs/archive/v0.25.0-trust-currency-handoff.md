# Handoff: Trust & Currency (phase 6) → v0.25.0

**Model: Opus.** Borderline, and it was close — M2/M3/M5 are well-specified
implementation that Sonnet would execute fine. **M4 decides it:** calibrating the
confidence floor means interpreting five eval sets that may not admit a single
satisfying value, and the correct output there is sometimes *"no default
satisfies 1–5, here is the trade-off"* rather than a number. A model that picks a
plausible number and moves on produces a green build and a worse engine, with no
test able to catch it. M1 (accepting or amending two compare-doc verdicts) is
judgment too.

**One-liner:** Fux ranks and answers by match, never by truth — it serves the
*superseded* answer 9 times in 12 and fabricates on every well-formed
out-of-scope question. This phase gives it a currency signal and an absolute
honesty floor.

**Owner / executor:** Claude Code
**Status:** Ready to build — **blocked only on Arpit accepting the two compare-doc
verdicts** (M1).

**Stress-tested:** Devil's advocate argued for fusion down-ranking (Option B in
`supersession-handling.compare.md`), on the fair ground that the measured harm is
at rank 1 of `find` and annotation does not touch it. It lost on evidence, not
mechanism: the penalty magnitude is a tuned number, and the repo's own filed
proposal already gates it on *a second realistic corpus* — set one day before
this handoff. Honoring an author-written `superseded_by:` needs no such evidence
because it is not a statistical judgment. A pre-mortem surfaced three failure
modes now mitigated in the DoD: an over-aggressive floor declining correct
answers (→ the five-way no-regression rule, DoD 6), supersession false-positives
on `status: draft` / prose mentions (→ exact-key parsing + near-miss tests,
DoD 3), and scope creep into down-ranking (→ §3 out-of-scope, explicit).
**Residual risk to watch:** annotation alone may not move real agent behaviour,
since `find` ordering is unchanged. That is measurable, and DoD 8 requires
measuring it rather than assuming either way.

## 1. Context & background

The acme-payments realistic corpus (`docs/conformance/2026-07-22-acme-payments/`)
settled the hybrid-degradation scare as a synthetic artifact — and then exposed
three real defects the synthetic tiers and the 21-pair fixture gate both missed.
Two of them are in scope here.

**Finding 1 — no currency signal (9/12 inversions).** An old ADR says settlement
is T+3; a newer one says T+2. Fux ranks the **retired** document at rank 1 and
the current one at rank 2, in 9 of 12 planted pairs. All three supersession
markers fail identically, because nothing in the pipeline knows one document
supersedes another — the markers are text it counts. BM25F length normalization
favors the terser legacy document, and the dense plane cannot break a tie between
near-identical topics (fused margin Δ 0.00079).

**Finding 3 — decline is too permissive (0/4).** `answer` declines gibberish but
fabricates on every well-formed out-of-scope question, returning confident
extractive prose with 1–5 citations from unrelated documents. The decline path
keys on an empty candidate pool; the admission threshold is relative, never
absolute.

Both are corpus-independent, both are deterministic to fix, and both are direct
threats to the product's central promise: *surface the answer that is still true,
or say you don't know.*

**Finding 2 (zero-overlap dense rescue, 0/6) is deliberately NOT here** — see §3.

## 2. Definition of done

- [ ] **DoD 1 — Verdicts accepted.** `supersession-handling.compare.md` and
      `answer-decline-floor.compare.md` are `status: accepted` (or amended per
      Arpit's call, and this handoff updated to match) before any code is written.
- [ ] **DoD 2 — Supersession parsed at ingest.** `status: superseded` and
      `superseded_by: <doc-id>` in frontmatter produce a persisted flag on the
      document in the substrate (`.fux/state/` and the db), surviving re-ingest
      byte-identically.
- [ ] **DoD 3 — Parsing is exact and tested.** Near-misses do **not** flag:
      `status: draft`, `status: superseded_by_nothing`, the word "superseded"
      appearing in body prose or a heading, a `superseded_by:` pointing at a
      non-existent doc (flag set, target recorded as unresolved — never a crash).
- [ ] **DoD 4 — Annotated in output, ordering unchanged.** `find` / `ask`
      `--json` carry `"superseded": true` and `"superseded_by": "<doc-id>"`;
      human output shows a marker. **The four `--lexical-only` goldens stay
      byte-identical** and result *ordering* is unchanged everywhere.
- [ ] **DoD 5 — `answer` prefers current.** When a superseded document and its
      named successor are both in the candidate pool, `answer` sources from the
      successor. When the successor is absent, it answers from what it has and
      annotates that the source is superseded.
- [ ] **DoD 6 — Confidence floor, calibrated, no regressions.** `[answer]
      min_confidence` exists in `fux.toml`. Its shipped default simultaneously:
      declines all 4 acme typed-unanswerables · still declines gibberish · causes
      **zero** regression on the 21-pair fixture gate, on acme's 55 answerable
      pairs, and on the synthetic 1k/5k/10k baselines. **If no value satisfies
      all five, ship the knob with a permissive default and report the measured
      trade-off — do not ship a default that declines correct answers.**
- [ ] **DoD 7 — `fux why` explains both.** Surfaces `superseded: true` (with the
      successor) and, on a decline, the reason with the numbers:
      `declined: best score 0.11 < min_confidence 0.20`.
- [ ] **DoD 8 — Measured, not assumed.** Re-run acme and report the before/after
      for: staleness inversions (was 9/12), `answer`-level staleness correctness,
      and unanswerable fabrication (was 0/4). Recovery on inversions is expected
      to be **partial** — only frontmatter-marked pairs are reachable. Report the
      real number; do not present partial recovery as a fix.
- [ ] **DoD 9 — Both suites green.** `tests/` and `tests_e2e/` pass; every
      behaviour change ships with a test.
- [ ] **DoD 10 — Docs updated** per §9.5, including the two ADRs.

## 3. Scope

**In scope:**

- Ingest-time frontmatter supersession parsing → substrate field.
- Output annotation in `find` / `ask` / `answer` (`--json` + human).
- `answer` preferring the un-superseded source.
- `[answer] min_confidence` absolute floor + calibration.
- `fux why` surfacing both signals.
- Two ADRs, docs, tests, changelog.

**Out of scope (explicit — do not "helpfully" add):**

- **Any change to `find` ranking or RRF fusion.** No supersession penalty, no
  re-weighting. This is the single most likely scope violation; it is Option B in
  the compare doc and it is deferred *on purpose*, gated on a second realistic
  corpus.
- **The runner-up margin check** on `answer` — Option B in the decline compare
  doc, deferred pending evidence of a class the floor cannot catch.
- **Chunk-level dense codes** (Finding 2). It is the structural fix for
  zero-overlap, it is real, and it belongs in its **own phase**: it changes the
  storage plane and puts the committed-state guarantee (~200 B/doc, a headline
  claim) at risk by a chunks-per-doc multiple. That needs its own compare doc and
  budget analysis. Not here.
- **NLP/heuristic detection** of dated-inline or unmarked supersession. Forbidden
  by the no-model constraint; accepted as a documented limit.
- Any use of a model anywhere in the maintenance path.

## 5. Technical approach (decided — do not re-litigate)

1. **Frontmatter is the contract.** Only machine-readable `status: superseded` /
   `superseded_by:` is acted on. Prose markers are out of reach without a model.
2. **Annotate, don't reorder.** Fux surfaces the fact deterministically; the
   consumer decides. This is what one corpus of evidence honestly supports.
3. **`answer` is where the preference lives** — a decline-to-current rule, not a
   ranking change to `find`.
4. **The floor is absolute, and it is one knob**, sitting *above* the existing
   zero-candidate early return (which stays — see the phase-4 note in CLAUDE.md
   about why removing it makes "No confident matches" unreachable).
5. **The default value is a measurement, not a choice.**

## 6. Non-negotiables / constraints

- **`$0`, stdlib-only runtime.** No third-party runtime dependency. Do not reach
  for PyYAML to parse the new frontmatter keys — extend the hand-rolled parser.
- **Deterministic — no model anywhere in the maintenance path.** Not for
  detecting supersession, not for judging answerability.
- **No wall-clock output.** Anything persisted derives from
  `SOURCE_DATE_EPOCH`/source mtime (ADR 0002); byte-identical re-ingest is a
  hard guarantee.
- **Python ≥ 3.11.** Match surrounding style and density.
- **The four `--lexical-only` goldens are byte-identical forever** (phase-4 law).
  Hybrid goldens change only with eval evidence.
- **Do not touch:** RRF fusion weights, BM25F parameters, the FuxVec code path,
  `archive/`.
- **Network-import fence** stays — nothing outside `ingest/` may import network
  modules, diagnostics included.
- **Docs style:** no large paragraphs; short points, 3–4 lines max, roomy.

## 8. Edge cases & risks

- `superseded_by:` naming a document that does not exist → flag set, target
  recorded unresolved, **no crash**, surfaced by `fux why`.
- Supersession **chains** (A → B → C) → follow to the end; **cycle detection
  required**, cycle logged and treated as unresolved rather than looping.
- A document superseded by *multiple* successors → record all; `answer` prefers
  any non-superseded one deterministically (stable order).
- The successor is itself superseded → prefer the terminal document.
- `min_confidence` set absurdly high → every question declines. Acceptable
  (explicit user choice), but `fux doctor` should flag a floor above a sane bound.
- Both documents in a stale pair are unmarked → **unfixable here**; must appear
  in the DoD 8 report as the residual, not be quietly excluded from the
  denominator.
- **Risk:** annotation alone does not change agent behaviour. Stated, measured
  in DoD 8, and the named reopen-trigger for Option B.
