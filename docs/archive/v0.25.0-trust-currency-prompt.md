# Claude Code prompt — Trust & Currency (phase 6) → v0.25.0

**Model: Opus.** Close call, and worth knowing why rather than defaulting.
M2/M3/M5 are well-specified implementation Sonnet would handle. **M4 decides
it:** calibrating the confidence floor means reading five eval sets that may not
admit any satisfying value, and the correct answer is sometimes *"no default
satisfies all five — here is the trade-off"*. A model that picks a plausible
number and proceeds produces a green build and a worse engine, and **no test
catches that**. M1 is judgment too.

You are implementing **phase 6 of Fux — "trust & currency"**: making the engine
stop confidently serving *retired* answers and stop *fabricating* on well-formed
out-of-scope questions.

The full spec is `docs/handoff/0006-trust-currency-handoff.md`. **Read it first
and treat its Definition of Done, Scope and Non-negotiables as binding.**

## Why this exists (one paragraph, then go read)

The acme-payments realistic corpus measured two defects the synthetic tiers and
the 21-pair fixture gate both missed. Fux ranks the **superseded** document above
the still-true one in **9 of 12** pairs — it has no notion of currency, only of
match. And `answer` **fabricates on all 4** well-formed unanswerable questions
(confident prose, 1–5 citations, unrelated documents), declining only gibberish.
Both are deterministic to fix. Neither requires a model.

## Constraints (hard)

- **`$0`, stdlib-only runtime.** No third-party runtime dependency. Extend the
  hand-rolled frontmatter parser — **do not** reach for PyYAML.
- **No model anywhere in the maintenance path.** Not for detecting supersession,
  not for judging answerability. Frontmatter is the only contract.
- **No wall-clock output** in anything persisted (ADR 0002).
- **Do NOT modify:** RRF fusion weights, BM25F parameters, the FuxVec code path,
  `archive/`.
- **Do NOT change `find` ranking.** No supersession penalty. This is the most
  likely scope violation in the whole phase — it is Option B in the compare doc
  and it is deferred deliberately, gated on a second realistic corpus.
- **Do NOT add the runner-up margin check.** Also deferred, also deliberate.
- **Do NOT build chunk-level dense codes** (the zero-overlap finding). Real, but
  it changes the storage plane and risks the ~200 B/doc committed-state
  guarantee. Its own phase, its own compare doc.
- Network-import fence holds — nothing outside `ingest/` imports network modules.
- Docs style: no large paragraphs; short points, 3–4 lines, roomy.
