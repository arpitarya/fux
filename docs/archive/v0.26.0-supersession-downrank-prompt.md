a penalty interval exists across four eval sets, and whether the margin check

## Why (one paragraph, then read)

v0.25.0 taught the engine to *recognise* a superseded document but not to *act on
it in `find`*. Two realistic corpora (acme 9/12, orbit 8/12 — independent domain)
show the retired document still ranking #1, annotation and all: in orbit, 5 of 6
frontmatter-reachable superseded docs carry the annotation *and* still outrank
their replacement. This phase adds a configurable fusion penalty — **default-off,
byte-identical to today** — calibrates it across every eval set, and reuses it to
give the refuted runner-up margin check a *clean* re-measurement (the smallest
"answerable" margins were a staleness artifact — superseded twins tying — so
removing them re-opens the Finding-2 question).
